Brisket

Do you want to experience something similar to franklins famous briskey. Check out this recipe!

to replicate this glorious creation of meatiness i will first provide you with a list of ingredients. Before i guide you through the process of making it.

ingredients

  • brisket, around 5-6kg
  • worchestershire sauce
  • brisket rub
  • lawrys seasoned salt
  • pellets

Directions

  1. Trim the brisket. make sure to trim off the thinnest part of the flat, since this part will dry out. the brisket should generally be made even and aerodynamic
  2. Lather the brisket in worchestershire sauce
  3. Apply a heavy layer of the peppery brisket rub. then apply a layer of the lawrys seasoning salt. fatty beef like this can handle quite a bit of salt.
  4. Pour the pellets into the smokers hopper and ignite.
  5. place the brisket into the smoker, next to the smoke outlet.
  6. Insert a water pan below the brisket to protect it from some of the direct heat, aswell as adding more moisture to the cook chamber.
  7. aim for a temperature of less than 100c for the first 4 hours of the cook
  8. after these hours we will be increasing the temperature to about 130c
  9. monitor the internal temperature. we will be extracing the brisket between 90c and 96c internal.
  10. hold the brisket in a 60c oven for atleast 12 hours
